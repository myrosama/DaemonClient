FAQ

Common questions.

How secure is my data?
All data is encrypted with zero-knowledge AES-GCM encryption — not even our developers can access your photos. Your encryption key never leaves your device. Only you can decrypt your library.
How does the app provide free storage?
DaemonClient Photos uses Telegram's infrastructure and Cloudflare's free tier as the backend. You set up a private Telegram channel, a Telegram bot, and a Cloudflare Worker with a D1 database. Your media lives in Telegram's servers (virtually unlimited storage per channel), and Cloudflare handles the API layer — both completely free, no credit card required.
Is DaemonClient Photos open source?
Yes. Every line of code that handles your photos is publicly available on GitHub. You can audit it, fork it, or self-host it entirely without any involvement from us.
Do I need technical skills to set it up?
Not much. Our step-by-step setup guide uses copy-paste commands and a browser-based dashboard for the Cloudflare and Telegram configuration. Most users are fully set up in under 15 minutes.
What happens to my photos if DaemonClient shuts down?
Your photos live in your own Telegram channel and Cloudflare account — DaemonClient never holds them. Even if we disappeared tomorrow, your library stays fully intact and accessible through Telegram directly.
Which devices are supported?
You can access your full library from any browser on Windows, Mac, or Linux. The mobile app (powered by Immich) supports iOS and Android with automatic background backup.
Is there really no storage limit?
No limit. Telegram provides virtually unlimited media storage per channel, and Cloudflare's free tier covers the database and API with generous allowances. We've had users store tens of thousands of photos with no issues.
